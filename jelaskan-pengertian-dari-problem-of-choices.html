4. Apkah definisi dari background of the study dan statement of the problem ?.


Answer:

In doing or making research on all kinds of field of studies, including English, those two parts are inseparable.
Background of study is the information on stuff which leads you to choose that main focus on your research. So basically it explains why you choose such focus and title of your final project or thesis. Example:
I did a research back then on the depiction of hedonism in F Scott Fitzgerald short stories. So the background of study is telling the reason why you choose those short stories, F Scott Fitzgerald' work and hedonism itself, ideally relate them to what happens today.
Statement of problems is the statement of your research focus. Usually you need to have two or three statement of problems, which later needs analysing and proving. In the example of mine, the statements I used were
- hedonism difference in both stories
- how the ideas of hedonism depicted in both stories.

Kelas: SMA
Mapel: English
Kategori : research

Background of study, is about the research on which you base your study problem, why are you doing it. A review paper gives an overview of research dealing with a specific topic. For instance, in our blog.
The background of a study is the information that a colleague who is in your field would need to know if they want to understand your research. It would give technical information and it would show that there are somethings that are not known.

A statement problem is one where certain conditions are stated, in a series of statements. The person solving the problem may have to do algebra equations, in order to solve the problem. There are also statements which must be solved logically. For example: 1) A man walks out of his front door, and walks a mile South. 2) He then walks a mile West, and shoots a bear. 3) He then walks a mile North, to his home. Question: What color is the bear?

20. causes of the problem


Jawaban:

The causes of a problem can vary widely depending on the specific issue or context. Identifying the causes of a problem is an important step in problem-solving and decision-making. Common causes of problems may include:

1. **Lack of Information:** Sometimes, problems arise because people lack necessary information or awareness about a situation, which can lead to misunderstandings or poor decision-making.

2. **Miscommunication:** Poor communication or miscommunication between individuals or groups can lead to conflicts, misunderstandings, and problems.

3. **Resource Constraints:** Limited resources, such as funding, manpower, or technology, can be a significant cause of problems in various situations.

4. **Environmental Factors:** Natural disasters, climate change, and other environmental factors can create problems in communities and organizations.

5. **Human Error:** Mistakes, oversight, or negligence by individuals can lead to problems, especially in safety-critical or high-risk environments.

6. **Technology Failures:** Problems can occur due to malfunctions or failures in technology, systems, or equipment.

7. **Economic Factors:** Economic conditions, like inflation, unemployment, or financial instability, can contribute to problems in businesses and societies.

8. **Cultural or Social Issues:** Differences in culture, values, or societal norms can lead to conflicts and problems in diverse communities.

9. **Legal and Regulatory Compliance:** Failure to comply with laws and regulations can result in problems for individuals, organizations, or governments.

10. **Political Factors:** Political decisions and policies can sometimes lead to problems, particularly when they are divisive or controversial.

11. **Health Issues:** Health problems and diseases can have a significant impact on individuals and communities, especially when there are outbreaks or pandemics.

12. **Historical or Systemic Factors:** Some problems have deep-rooted causes related to historical events, systemic issues, or long-standing inequalities.

Identifying the specific causes of a problem is essential to finding effective solutions and addressing the root issues. It often involves careful analysis, research, and understanding of the context in which the problem occurs.

40. pengertian manifest social problem dan latent social problem


Manifest social problem dan latent social problem merupakan bentuk dari masalah sosial :

Manifest social problem merupakan masalah sosial yang timbul akibat ketidaksesuaian anatara tindakan dan nilai serta norma sosial dimana dampaknya langsung dirasakan oleh masyarakat.

Latent social problem merupakan masalah sosial yang tersembunyi sehingga dampaknya tidak dirasakan secara langsung oleh masyarakat.

Pembahasan

Masalah sosial adalah bentuk problematika yang terjadi dalam kehidupan masyarakat yang disebabkan adanya ketidak seimbangan interaksi sosial antara satu dengan lainnya.

Faktor masalah sosial :

1. Faktor Psikologis , yaitu masalah sosial yang berhubungan dengan gangguan kejiwaan seseorang.

2. Faktor biologi, yaitu masalah sosial yang berhubungan dengan fisik seseorang seperti kesehatan atau suatu penyakit.

3. Faktor budaya, yaitu masalah yang  berkaitan dengan norma atu unsur - unsur nilai sosial seperti adat istiadat dan kebiasaan masyarakat.

4. Faktor ekonomi, yaitu masalah sosial yang berkaitan dengan masalah, kekayaan, pendapatan, pekerjaan dan upaya pemenuhan kebutuhan hidup.

Dampak masalah sosial :

1. Semakin meningkatnya angka kriminalitas.

2. Kenyamanan, ketentraman dan keamanan masyarakat menjadi terganggtu.

3.  Menimbulkan kerusakan fisik seperti sarana dan prasarana, fasilitas umum.
